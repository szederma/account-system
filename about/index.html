About Us

Three 14-Year-Old Students from Hungary Invent a New Musical Instrument?

We are Matyas Szederkenyi, Dora Szederkenyi, and Laura Kürtösi — three students from the German School of Budapest. We were in the eighth grade when we began working on what would later become the Distanzuino project. It all started a year earlier, in seventh grade, when we joined our school’s physics and robotics club. That’s where we first discovered the world of technology and programming — and from that moment on, we knew we wanted to create something of our own.

The idea began to take shape when we decided to enter the Jugend forscht competition. Since we’re all passionate about music, we knew we wanted to merge our two biggest interests: music and technology. Our concept evolved through countless experiments — and failures — until the Distanzuino was born: a digital musical instrument based on distance measurement.

Being long-time classmates and friends made teamwork natural and enjoyable. We quickly realized that we not only had a creative idea, but also the determination and skills to turn it into a working prototype.

Our goal wasn’t just to invent something new — we wanted to create a tool that helps young people explore music and learn the basics of technology in a fun, interactive way. We believe that these are essential skills for the future, and Distanzuino offers a playful introduction to both.

With this project, we’ve participated in several national and international competitions including Jugend Forscht and OTIO (Hungarian National Olympiad for Science and Innovation). Our mission now is to make the Distanzuino available to as many young people as possible — so they can experience the joy of making music while learning about technology, sensors, and creative thinking.
